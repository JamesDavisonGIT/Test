Arrangement notes

Key: D minor. The song should begin intimate and slightly ominous, then become clearer and more spiritual by the final chorus.

Main progression: Dm – Bb – F – C. This gives the song a familiar emotional pull: dark root, warm lift, open release, unresolved return.

Pre-chorus: Bb – C – Dm – A7. The A7 adds harmonic pressure back into D minor and makes the “rootkit” section feel like a system warning.

Bridge: Gm – Bb – F – C – Gm – A7. This temporarily leaves the loop so the philosophy section feels like a new permissions layer.

Vocal range: mostly C4–C5 with occasional D5 in choruses. If D5 feels strained, transpose the entire song down to C minor or B minor.

Production concept: first chorus should feel ambiguous, almost like temptation. Final chorus should use the same melodic DNA but feel like reclaimed command.
